## Overview of Representative Models by Generation

- **Gen 1 (approx. 1982–1987)**

    - Representative: **Glock 17** (the first model released, prototype of the Glock series).

- **Gen 2 (approx. 1988–1997)**

    - Common/models introduced or improved to Gen2 style during this period: **G17 (Gen2)**, **G18** (select-fire/auto model, released around 1987/1988), **G19** (first mass-produced and popularized during this period), etc.

- **Gen 3 (approx. 1998–2009)**

    - Numerous models widely produced in Gen3 style: **G17 / G19 / G20 / G21 / G22 / G23 / G26 / G27 / G34 / G35**, etc. (Gen3 is one of the most popular Glock generations).

- **Gen 4 (approx. 2010–2016)**

    - Built on Gen3 foundation with interchangeable backstraps and dual recoil spring: Common Gen4 models include **G17 Gen4, G19 Gen4, G22 Gen4, G26 Gen4, G34 Gen4**, etc. (multiple Gen4 versions gradually released from 2010).

- **Gen 5 (2017–Present)**

    - Glock released Gen5 in 2017 with improved barrel (Marksman Barrel), removed finger grooves, ambidextrous slide release, etc. Representative/Gen5 versions include: **G17 Gen5, G19 Gen5, G26 Gen5, G34 Gen5, G19X/G45/G43X/48 (some are Gen5 design or hybrid)**. Official also extended Gen5 to various calibers and MOS/special editions.
